## üìê ANIMAL POV REFERENCE GUIDE

**KEY PRINCIPLES:**
- **Eye Height**: Match exact animal eye level from ground (cm)
- **Movement Pattern**: Replicate natural gait (walking, running, flying, swimming)
- **Head Movement**: Include species-specific head bobbing, swaying, or stabilization
- **Field of View**: Consider eye position (side-mounted vs front-facing)
- **Speed**: Match natural locomotion speed (slow waddle vs fast sprint)

**CAMERA BEHAVIOR:**
- Handheld to simulate natural body movement
- Height locked to animal's eye level
- Movement speed matches animal gait
- Head bobbing/swaying based on species
- Include breathing rhythm in camera shake

## üêæ ANIMAL SPECIFICATIONS

### DOMESTIC ANIMALS

**CAT (House Cat)**
- Eye Height: 25-30cm
- Movement: Smooth prowl 0.3-1 ft/sec, graceful head stabilization
- Head Motion: Minimal vertical bob (2-3cm), focused forward gaze
- FOV: 200¬∞ peripheral (eyes slightly forward)
- Gait: Silent paw placement, fluid hip movement
- Speed: Prowl 0.5 ft/sec | Walk 1 ft/sec | Run 8-12 ft/sec

**DOG (Medium breed - Labrador)**
- Eye Height: 45-55cm
- Movement: Energetic trot 1.5-3 ft/sec, rhythmic head bob
- Head Motion: Vertical bob 5-8cm per stride, panting rhythm
- FOV: 240¬∞ peripheral (eyes more lateral)
- Gait: Four-beat trot, tail-influenced balance
- Speed: Walk 2 ft/sec | Trot 4-6 ft/sec | Run 15-20 ft/sec

**RABBIT**
- Eye Height: 15-20cm (sitting) | 8-12cm (running)
- Movement: Hop pattern, pause-and-scan behavior
- Head Motion: Ears pivot independently, nose twitches 2-3 times/sec
- FOV: 360¬∞ nearly complete (eyes on sides of head)
- Gait: Explosive hops 1-2 ft forward, freeze between hops
- Speed: Hop 2-4 ft/sec | Escape run 10-15 ft/sec

### WILD ANIMALS

**LION**
- Eye Height: 90-100cm
- Movement: Powerful prowl 0.5-2 ft/sec, shoulder roll gait
- Head Motion: Heavy head sway 8-10cm side-to-side, predator focus
- FOV: 200¬∞ forward-focused (predator eyes)
- Gait: Diagonal walking pattern, muscular shoulder movement
- Speed: Stalk 0.5 ft/sec | Walk 2 ft/sec | Sprint 25-30 ft/sec

**ELEPHANT**
- Eye Height: 200-250cm
- Movement: Slow deliberate walk 1-2 ft/sec, swaying trunk
- Head Motion: Massive side-to-side sway 15-20cm, trunk movement
- FOV: 180¬∞ with trunk awareness below
- Gait: Slow rolling shoulder motion, ground tremor feel
- Speed: Walk 2 ft/sec | Charge 10-12 ft/sec

**MOUSE**
- Eye Height: 3-5cm
- Movement: Rapid darting 1-3 ft/sec, wall-hugging behavior
- Head Motion: Constant twitching, whisker contact feedback
- FOV: 320¬∞ panoramic (eyes on sides)
- Gait: Scurrying with rapid paw placement
- Speed: Explore 1 ft/sec | Escape 3-5 ft/sec

**EAGLE (Flying)**
- Eye Height: 500-3000cm (variable altitude)
- Movement: Soaring glide or active flapping
- Head Motion: Gyroscopic stabilization (head remains fixed while body tilts)
- FOV: 340¬∞ with telescopic central focus
- Gait: Wing beats 2-3 per second (active) | Still gliding (soaring)
- Speed: Glide 8-12 ft/sec | Dive 60-80 ft/sec

**DOLPHIN (Swimming)**
- Eye Height: 30-50cm above water surface (when surfacing)
- Movement: Undulating swim 3-6 ft/sec, breach and re-entry
- Head Motion: Smooth aquatic glide, echolocation scanning
- FOV: 280¬∞ underwater, wide peripheral
- Gait: Tail fluke propulsion, rhythmic undulation
- Speed: Cruise 5 ft/sec | Fast swim 15-20 ft/sec

### BIRDS

**PIGEON (Ground)**
- Eye Height: 20-25cm
- Movement: Head-bobbing walk 0.5-1.5 ft/sec
- Head Motion: Extreme forward-back bob 8-10cm with each step
- FOV: 340¬∞ almost complete panoramic
- Gait: Jerky head stabilization, feet alternate rapidly
- Speed: Walk 1 ft/sec | Peck-walk 0.5 ft/sec

**OWL (Perched/Flying)**
- Eye Height: 40-60cm (perched) | Variable (flying)
- Movement: Silent flight or fixed perch position
- Head Motion: Can rotate 270¬∞, locks onto target
- FOV: 110¬∞ binocular forward (predator), head rotation compensates
- Gait: Near-silent wing beats, head remains stabilized
- Speed: Glide 4-6 ft/sec | Attack dive 15-20 ft/sec

## üé¨ MOVEMENT PATTERNS BY SPECIES

**QUADRUPED WALK (Cat, Dog, Lion):**
- Four-beat gait: right front, left rear, left front, right rear
- Head bobs with shoulder movement
- Spine flexion creates slight pitch variation

**HOPPING (Rabbit, Kangaroo):**
- Explosive rear leg push-off
- Airborne phase (camera lifts 10-30cm)
- Landing impact (slight camera shake)
- Pause phase (stillness, scanning)

**AVIAN FLIGHT (Eagle, Owl, Pigeon):**
- Head gyroscopically stabilized (remains level)
- Body tilts for turns, head stays fixed
- Wing beats create subtle rhythmic bounce
- Gliding = smooth steady movement

**SWIMMING (Dolphin, Seal):**
- Undulating sine-wave motion
- Surface breaches = water-to-air transition
- Underwater = muffled blue-green tint
- Breathing rhythm visible in surface behavior

## üéØ LIGHTING & ENVIRONMENT FROM ANIMAL PERSPECTIVE

**GROUND-LEVEL ANIMALS (Cat, Dog, Mouse):**
- Lower viewpoint sees underside of furniture, grass blades tower
- Shadows loom larger
- Human legs/feet prominent in frame
- Floor textures highly detailed

**TALL ANIMALS (Giraffe, Elephant):**
- Overlook obstacles humans can't see past
- Treetops at eye level
- Human heads below eye line
- Wide environmental context

**FLYING ANIMALS (Eagle, Owl):**
- Landscape patterns visible (roads, rivers, fields)
- Depth perception from altitude
- Thermal vision effect (for some raptors)
- Cloud proximity

**NOCTURNAL ANIMALS (Owl, Cat):**
- Enhanced low-light sensitivity (brighter darks)
- Motion-sensitive vision
- Reduced color saturation (more monochromatic)
- Glow from reflective surfaces (tapetum lucidum effect for cats)

## ‚öôÔ∏è TECHNICAL EXECUTION TIPS

**Camera Rigging:**
- Use actual height measurements (not approximate)
- Lock camera to exact eye height throughout shot
- Match movement speed precisely (ft/sec)
- Include species-specific head motion

**Movement Animation:**
- Study real animal locomotion videos
- Include weight transfer in gait
- Add breathing rhythm to camera movement
- Species-specific: bobbing vs stabilization

**Environmental Scale:**
- Adjust all objects relative to animal height
- Ground textures more prominent for low POV
- Sky/ceiling more prominent for tall POV
- Human objects appear different scale

**Lighting Considerations:**
- Lower POV = more shadows from above
- Higher POV = overlook shadow areas
- Nocturnal = enhanced low-light sensitivity
- Aquatic = blue-green color shift
